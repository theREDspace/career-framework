---
code: SSD2
position: Senior Software Developer
tier: ssd
theme: Grows
description: Expanding impact across teams and systems
order: 4
competencies:
  technology:
    keyword: Evangelist
    summary: Evaluates and selects technologies by performing structured trade-off analyses and ensures decisions mitigate long-term technical debt.
    bullets:
      - Understands and makes well-reasoned decisions regarding technologies and frameworks that have not yet been adopted by the company
      - Evaluates new tech (e.g., AI integration, new languages) and brings proven, researched alternatives to the leadership team
      - Expertise is sought out specifically for high-risk problems and evaluating significant shifts in technical direction
      - Acts as an go-to expert for their primary tech stack. They are the final escalation point for the most complex, "impossible" bugs in that domain.
      - Deeply understands the internal workings of their platform (memory management, rendering engines, store submission guidelines) and ensures the company avoids "toxic" technical debt unique to that stack.
      - Evaluates and optimizes the impact of AI tools on the development lifecycle. Identifies domains where AI implementation poses high risk (e.g., core security primitives or complex distributed locking) and establishes guardrails to maintain engineering rigor.
  solution:
    keyword: Owns
    summary: Owns major parts of the system with minimal oversight.
    bullets:
      - Authoritatively decides on architectural paths that are difficult or expensive to reverse, ensuring they align with client and business goals
      - Coordinates technical solutions that require integration between disparate groups or services
      - Leads the discovery process for large-scale initiatives, identifying risks that could impact the company’s reputation or long-term budget
      - Ensures that owned systems meet high-performance benchmarks and quality standards consistently
      - Owns the technical execution path for high-complexity, high-risk projects within their domain. Ensures the architecture is resilient enough to handle future client requirements.
      - Implements advanced patterns (caching strategies, error boundaries, offline support) that become the blueprint for others in that same stack.
  people:
    keyword: Coordinates
    summary: Identifies and solves effective growth solutions for the team.
    bullets:
      - Identifies and resolves communication bottlenecks between teams or departments
      - Coaches SSD1s on how to be better coaches, scaling mentorship across the organization
      - Effectively facilitates communication and alignment with external stakeholders or different technical disciplines
      - Leads code challenges and system design interviews, moving beyond the "script" to probe a candidate's depth of knowledge
  process:
    keyword: Definer
    summary: Coordinates process alignment across groups.
    bullets:
      - Authors and implements development processes that are adopted across multiple teams or the entire discipline
      - Owns high-stakes, 'One-Way Door' decisions where reversal is costly; ensures these foundational workflows provide long-term stability for the entire organization.
      - Identifies and defines the "interface" between departments (e.g., how Dev interacts with Creative or QA) to ensure seamless handoffs
      - Defines the standard tooling and automation architecture (CI/CD, environment management) that all teams are expected to use
  influence:
    keyword: Transforms
    summary: Introduces entirely new service offerings or technical capabilities to the company.
    bullets:
      - While working on a single client, they build systems, tools, or frameworks in a way that is intentionally modular, allowing other REDspace teams to "harvest" that logic for different clients
      - A trusted "Subject Matter Expert" that other SSDs or Directors call upon to vet architectures or solve "impossible" bugs on other accounts
      - Elevates REDspace's standing with the client from "vendor" to "strategic partner." Their influence ensures the client looks to REDspace for the next big technical pivot
      - Their primary "Transformation" is the caliber of the team they leave behind. They ensure that their project can run with high-level maturity even if they were to step away
      - "They may only work on Client A, but they proactively communicate to REDspace leadership: \"I'm seeing [X] problem here, and I suspect our other entertainment/streaming clients are hitting this too. Here is how we should handle it as a company.\""
      - Influence is felt across the entire discipline. While they aren't managing the whole company's strategy, every developer in their discipline looks to them for "The Way" things should be built.
      - Sets the bar for Code Reviews and documentation within their domain, ensuring that "Good Enough" is replaced by "Engineering Excellence."
      - Socializes "State of the Union" updates for their stack, ensuring other developers are aware of breaking industry changes or new best practices
  client:
    keyword: Ownership
    summary: Aligns technical choices with client’s long-term goals.
    bullets:
      - Makes architectural decisions that account for where the client’s business is going in 18–24 months, preventing expensive rewrites later
      - Knows when it's appropriate to "move fast and break things" for a marketing launch and when it's critical to slow down for stability
      - Understands how their project fits into the client’s larger ecosystem (e.g., how this app interacts with the client’s 5 other internal systems)
      - Proactively identifies security, compliance, or scalability issues that could hurt the client’s brand in the future
---
