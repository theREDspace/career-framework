---
code: SSD1
position: Senior Software Developer
tier: ssd
theme: Leads
description: Leading through technical mastery and mentorship
order: 3
competencies:
  technology:
    keyword: Lifecycle Master
    summary: Knowledge of the whole technology development lifecycle.
    bullets:
      - Masters the whole technology Development Life Cycle (SDLC), allowing them to ramp up on new technologies quickly by applying foundational architectural patterns
      - Possesses very deep knowledge of the current tech stack while maintaining the "technical fluency" to lead diverse projects across the company
      - Designs systems and solutions with advanced resiliency patterns (e.g., circuit breakers, retries, and graceful degradation) to ensure service continuity during partial outages
      - Acts as a primary unblocker for complex technical problems that cross multiple project boundaries
      - Understands performance and infrastructure (how code compiles, memory footprints, performance limiations, etc.) to ensure long-term system health
      - Provides the stability needed to "right the ship" or build a new foundation
      - Acts as a high-integrity reviewer for AI-assisted contributions. Ensures the team doesn't sacrifice long-term maintainability for short-term velocity by rigorously verifying that agentic implementations adhere to team architectural patterns and security standards.
  solution:
    keyword: Evolves
    summary: Refactors existing systems for long-term maintainability.
    bullets:
      - Takes ownership of existing systems, refactoring and improving them for long-term maintainability rather than just adding features
      - Ensures the system as a whole degrades gracefully during failure
      - Provides data-driven recommendations on when to 'patch' versus 'rebuild' specific project components, balancing immediate business timelines with long-term technical health; escalates decisions with organizational-wide impact to higher-tier staff.
      - Reduces the cognitive load for the rest of the team by taking complex requirements and breaking them into simple, manageable architectures
      - While they might not be the primary architect for the whole company, they are a primary authority on the technical execution path for their project
  people:
    keyword: Coaches
    summary: Formally mentors a peer to their next career level.
    bullets:
      - Uses code reviews and technical discussions as a platform to explain concepts, not just correct syntax
      - Actively seeks out and listens to diverse perspectives within the team
      - Improves the overall output of the team by identifying and fixing skill gaps in others
      - Authors documentation or runbooks specifically designed to help others master a process or system
      - Conducts technical interviews independently, providing high-signal, objective feedback on a candidate’s technical and cultural fit
  process:
    keyword: Evolver
    summary: Expands the development lifecycle by identifying legacy process debt and replacing it with modern, automated standards.
    bullets:
      - Defines the "Definition of Done" for the project, ensuring that quality is an evolved, measurable standard rather than a moving target
      - Enforces project-wide discipline, acting as the guardian of the newly evolved workflows to prevent regression into old habits
      - Able to shift or delegate tasks as necessary to ensure the success of the project
      - Optimizes the Path to Production, evolving manual deployment steps into automated CI/CD "Standards"
      - Authors the "Source of Truth", creating the documentation that allows the team's process to evolve from "tribal knowledge" to canonical standards
  influence:
    keyword: Catalyzes
    summary: Drives team-wide standards and refactors. About Awareness and Cross-Pollination.
    bullets:
      - Identifies when "the way we've always done it" is no longer the best path and provides a viable, evidence-based alternative
      - Influences the department’s reputation by setting a high bar for quality that others naturally strive to emulate
      - Effectively communicates the value of technical initiatives to stakeholders, ensuring that "Evolving" projects get the resources and time they need
      - Ensures that influence is used to pull others up, creating spaces for more junior voices to be heard in high-level discussions
  client:
    keyword: Trust
    summary: Acts as a strategic partner; offers technical tradeoffs.
    bullets:
      - Doesn't just say "yes" or "no." Explains and understands the cost/benefit of different paths (e.g., "We can build this custom for $20k, or use an off-the-shelf tool for $5k with these limitations")
      - Proactively suggests ways to achieve the same business goal with less technical complexity
      - Understands the client’s business pressures (deadlines, budget cycles, stakeholders) and aligns technical delivery to support them (Technical Empathy)
      - Capable and trusted to lead technical demos and discussions with the client, maintaining a calm and professional demeanor even when discussing bugs or delays
---
