---
code: SD1
position: Software Developer
tier: sd
theme: Adopts
description: Learning the fundamentals and building good habits
order: 0
competencies:
  technology:
    keyword: Adopts
    summary: Actively learns and adopts the technology and tools defined by the team.
    bullets:
      - Follows team syntax and library standards without repeated corrections
      - Demonstrates knowledge of basic development principles
      - Manages local environment and follows version control workflows
      - Focuses on growing as a developer, learning existing tools, resources and processes
      - Shows an eagerness to learn and cultivate their knowledge base
  solution:
    keyword: Enhances
    summary: Fixes bugs and adds small features under guidance.
    bullets:
      - Builds functions to improve and add to existing services
      - Executes well-defined tasks under guidance, focusing on code quality and following established patterns
      - Learns to timebox independent problem-solving and proactively utilizes team resources or peers to get unblocked after reasonable effort.
      - Follows established testing norms, ensuring their work meets the team’s definition of “done”
      - Understands why their task is part of the system through explanation
      - If using AI assistants, the engineer must deeply understand the suggestions/implementations created by agents and be able to explain why a specific solution was chosen over alternatives.
      - Demonstrates growing awareness of how their code impacts the larger subsystem
  people:
    keyword: Learns
    summary: Actively seeks guidance and learns from others to improve their own performance and understanding.
    bullets:
      - Engages in team ceremonies (standups, retros, code review) and contributes to a positive team environment
      - Responds to feedback professionally and keeps the team informed of their progress or blockers
      - Shows an eagerness to learn not just the tech, but the interpersonal dynamics of a professional dev team
      - Actively participates in pairing sessions as the "observer" or "student" to learn best practices
  process:
    keyword: Adopter
    summary: Follows established rules.
    bullets:
      - Executes promptly and stays on task once a workflow has been defined by the team
      - Learns and adopts the development, testing, and shipping processes used by the project team
      - Follows team syntax and library standards without requiring repeated corrections
      - Updates status tools (like JIRA) consistently, ensuring the team knows where their tasks stand
      - Adheres to version control hygiene, including following naming conventions for branches and writing clear, descriptive commit messages
  influence:
    keyword: Participates
    summary: Contributes to team discussions and ceremonies (standups, retros), ensuring their voice is part of the collective effort.
    bullets:
      - Impacts the immediate subsystem by completing tasks promptly and accurately
      - Builds a reputation for reliability by meeting deadlines and communicating blockers early
      - Aligns their work habits with the cultural and technical standards defined by the team
      - Learns to advocate for their work, can clearly explain what they built and why during code reviews or internal demos
  client:
    keyword: Reliability
    summary: Delivers "Correct" code.
    bullets:
      - Ensures that the code delivered matches the acceptance criteria (AC) provided in the ticket
      - Shows a commitment to quality by self-testing their work before handing it off, reducing the time spent in the QA/Dev loop
      - Represents REDspace well in internal meetings by being prepared, punctual, and attentive to project goals
      - Understands that even small bug fixes or minor features contribute to the overall health of the client’s product
      - Prioritizes getting the implementation "right" (per the spec) over rushing a solution that might be technically broken
---
