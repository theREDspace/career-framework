---
code: SQA1
position: Senior QA Specialist
tier: sqa
theme: Evangelizes
description: Driving quality practices and mentoring others
order: 2
competencies:
  technology:
    keyword: Evangelizes
    summary: Researches, introduces new technologies and concepts. 
    bullets:
      - Has expanded to practicing observability checks and root cause analysis
      - Is actively introducing themselves to automated testing tools and frameworks
      - Regularly fields questions from other testers that recognize them as deeply experienced in a given technology
      - Asks meaningful questions about assumptions or inconsistencies during planning and grooming phases
      - Demonstrates authoritative ownership of projects, consistently delivering results that build stakeholder trust and generate ongoing demand for their leadership.
      - Understands the benefits of deeper technical understanding of the Software Under Test and begins to mentor others
      - Shows confidence with source control tooling - will critically review and question what testing must be conducted and what is wasteful as code changes propagate further along toward production
  solution:
    keyword: Owns
    summary: Owns test case management, test documentation, and understands when automation should be implemented.
    bullets:
      - Persistent in the face of challenges and roadblocks
      - Investigates issues and does root cause analysis on issues
      - Troubleshoots before reporting a bug to ensure it is not environmental, backend, or API related
      - Approaches problem solving effectively, pulling in others as necessary
      - Given a task that is harder to complete, or very involved, attempts novel techniques to complete tasks, and disseminates info on the task
      - Meaningfully participates in and drives critical discussions on Application Design, User Experience, and any other aspects of test
      - Proactively identifies problems with requirements (lack of clarity, inconsistencies, design/UX issues) and communicates these issues early to help course-correct
  people:
    keyword: Mentors
    summary: Mentors others to level up their skills and encourages them to grow.
    bullets:
      - Represents the business professionally when speaking with clients and partners
      - Coaches and mentors other non-senior QA staff, offering technical support and guidance
      - Demonstrates excellent interpersonal skills including strong written and verbal communication
      - Gives and receives feedback effectively, positively, and professionally
      - Exhibits confidence, clarity, and knowledge when interfacing with staff and clients
      - Demonstrates ability to create documents for training purposes
      - Thoughtfully wagers credibility as a Senior when interacting with others
  process:
    keyword: Challenges
    summary: Challenges the team processes, seeking ways to improve them.
    bullets:
      - Raises constructive feedback with regards to project process
      - Actively contributes during Project Retros to help shape and improve the process
      - Understands the impact of feedback, and when to provide it in an effective and productive way
      - Contributes regularly to improving the process flow and is able to explain it to new people on the project
      - Can onboard people onto projects or the company
      - Finds gaps in testing or process and sees how the team could better utilize existing tools
  influence:
    keyword: Multiple Teams
    summary: Makes an impact not only on the whole team but also on other teams.
    bullets:
      - Provides feedback that is beneficial to both parties, often communicating with developers, leads, and PMs about adjusting flow (build tickets, better bug tracking, additional analytics to help with crash reporting, etc)
      - Has enough knowledge that they are able to show everything and explain everything comprehensively so another party can understand
      - Demonstrates critical thinking around encountering bugs - can deduce root causes, identify whether issues are environmental, backend/API, or frontend, and determine who can resolve problems to unblock testing
  client:
    keyword: Autonomy
    summary: Proactively identifies quality risks and gaps in requirements.
    bullets:
      - Doesn't just start testing; they ask the clarifying questions during grooming that expose missing requirements and testability gaps before development begins
      - Takes ambiguous business requests and identifies the test scenarios that will prove the feature delivers real value, saving the client time and money
      - Proactively identifies dependencies with other teams or third-party vendors that could impact quality early in the process
      - Offers small, immediate suggestions to improve the user experience or test coverage while staying within the original scope
      - Can be given a high-level goal and is trusted to return with a comprehensive test strategy that covers all the bases
---
